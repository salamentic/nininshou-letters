When it comes to music, I’m not necessarily what would be considered an amateur, so to some extent, I can do some score reading for you. Notes being written on anything other than sheet music evoke a slightly curious sensation, doesn’t it? As if you were decorating an object, an image of letters embellished by sounds came to mind. 　About “Even the Flowers Rustle” This is a poem about delighting in summer’s arrival, if I’m not mistaken. I noticed you used the word “white cloth (shirotae)” numerous times. And further, you keep using vocabulary like cloud and sleeve, so I presume that you're using "white cloth (shirotae)" as a pillow word (allusive poetic epithet). The white cloth, when aligned with the phrase “Hidden is the moon that ought to shine,” it would almost feel like a quote from the Man'yōshū. Perhaps it’s this one: “Is it the white-clad clouds that hide the moon that should shine clear upon the mirror? Or is it the heavenly mist?” I don’t have it on hand right now so I can’t say for sure. In the past, there was a kind of textile created from tree bark called “tae”. The word shirotae is, in other words, “white cloth,” and is used to describe garments made of such materials.
