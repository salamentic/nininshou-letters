I used to sneak into my mother’s study often. Most of what was there were specialized books I couldn’t really understand (or are they technical books? I wonder which is correct).

Either is fine.

But occasionally there were novels and poetry collections as well. Among them, my favorite works were those of Miyazawa Kenji. In his works, the ones that were easier to understand and closer to children’s literature were printed as thin, small booklets for each story, which made them easy for me to pick up as a child. I think there were “The Nighthawk Star”, “The Restaurant of Many Orders”, and “Gauche the Cellist”.As the number of books I had finished grew and the choices that interested me became fewer, there was one thing I still hesitated to pick up poetry collections for a long time.

That’s surprising

Yes, although now I’m drawn to poetry, to be honest it was something I didn’t really understand at all. And then, in one of the poetry collections I finally picked up, I found the word “Shura”. And I thought “This is it”. But it frightened me. It felt as though I were being shown myself, as if looking into a mirror.
