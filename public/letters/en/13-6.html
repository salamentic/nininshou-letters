June 30

When I was younger, I hurt someone. It was a physical wound. Even for a fight between children it was a large, unforgettable memory. Back then, I even had friends and we’d play on my way back home from school, run around empty lots on holidays, and I was able to enjoy playing with them. But one day, whatever happened, I had a fight with those friends. I seem to recall that it was about a really trivial thing. I got so awfully angry, then unable to bear it, I pushed him back. It’s not like I seriously wanted to hurt him. It was an impulsive act. The place my friend had fallen on was a curb. So his head was stricken and blood streamed down. An awfully large gash had appeared on his forehead, and a faintly white bone could be seen. Then I, having realised that I’d done something that could never be undone, ran away frightened.
