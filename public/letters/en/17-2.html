The smell strengthened in this part. When I lit the match, the scent of phosphorus appeared. I lit the cigarette and watched it. The burning tip pulsed rhythmically, like a heartbeat. Like something eaten away by a bug. The burnt portion gradually grows smaller. Unable to bear its own weight, the ash began to droop its neck. It resembled my mother's silhouette a little. Since it had burnt out to the base, I crushed it to the ground, putting out the flame. Like a pencil shaving, the tobacco leaves were thin, crumbling away in overflowing brown dregs when I twisted it. With that, I think I understand why people smoke cigarettes.
