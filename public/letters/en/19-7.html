Regarding point 1: In Chinese, there are traditional characters and simplified characters. Traditional characters were typically used for writing for a long time, and simplified characters are simplified versions of them designed to make reading and writing easier. What you have written are the simplified characters. Obviously, the original text written by Yuan Zhen used traditional characters, so the correct way to write it is this way:   曾經滄海難爲水   除卻巫山不是雲 Regarding point 2: You could say this is a simple metaphor for the desire for knowledge. Since ancient times, the snake has been associated with longevity in China; it was an animal that symbolized wisdom. In ancient culture, you will find many books where snakes symbolize immortality and resurrection. Snakes shed their skins, don’t they? I suppose that when people in ancient times witnessed this process of rejuvenation, they thought it was a mystery, don’t you think? Beings that live for a long time possess natural wisdom, which is why snakes are also associated with knowledge. In reality, the act of shedding one’s skin is not an act of rejuvenation; it is simply aging.
