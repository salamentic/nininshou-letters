August 18

I noticed that lately you've been including pages for me to reply on. Thank you so much. I'm sorry if I've caused you any trouble. That’s because I’ve often imagined my mother as a female figure. You once told me to try writing about myself, didn’t you? Over the long time that has passed, an image came to mind. It’s a woman dancing in a place where there’s nothing. Her face can’t be seen. But somehow, I know she’s my mother. It’s just my imagination because I don’t like to write about people close to me in a very direct way. So, maybe I wrote about her enjoying the moment as much as possible. My mother always looks unhappy. Even though my grandfather helps her out from time to time, she’s raising me as a single mother. In the mornings, she leaves the house before I wake up, and she comes home late at night. That’s probably not because we’re poor. She has a study room, and she’s never told me about any financial problems. Maybe she’s just putting up a strong front. My mother lives as if time is pressing down on her, and she always has a serious look on her face. She doesn’t say anything to me. She really doesn’t talk to me about anything.

 Tell me about your mother. It's fine if you do it indirectly, like you did in this letter. Do you know the word “dessin”? It means observing something you saw somewhere and, based on that, drawing a sketch of it.
