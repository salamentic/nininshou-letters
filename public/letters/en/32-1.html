Dear Sensei, This will be the last letter, as well as the last poem I send. You don’t have to reply to this letter; not an opinion nor a word. I have merely sent this with the desire for you to see it. There is no reference. Though, if words themselves are a collection of references, then perhaps that’s not entirely true. But that’s not what I mean. I’m not sure how to put it; this is just what is inside myself. Just simply the words from within me. I’ve been thinking about these past 9 months. Rereading the letters, looking at the manuscript papers that you sent back. And now, I’m certain of a possibility. My mother said that Sensei is an acquaintance, but would someone really entrust their child’s correspondence to a mere acquaintance? I’ve heard that in principle, prisons don’t allow visitation from anyone beyond relatives within the sixth degree of kinship. Of course there has to be exceptions.
