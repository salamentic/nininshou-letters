"Devilishness." It's a fine phrase. What first comes to my mind is a woman: there's a term called "femme fatale", right? It literally means "fatal woman," and it's used to describe a woman with such devilish charm as to lead men's lives to ruin. A famous example is Salome by Oscar Wilde. It’s known for the scene where, unable to accept that John the Baptist will never be hers, she demands that his head be brought to her. Before “Salome”, she originally appeared as a princess in the New Testament. However, there was a time within literary circles that it became popular to reinterpret her as a figure of destructive, bewitching beauty. It’s said that Wilde took much inspiration from painter Gustave Moreau’s depictions of Salome. If you’d like, you could look for the painting in the library too. It’s provocative, but nevertheless a beautiful work of art. That’s what I see when I think of “devilishness.” And I read your poem.
