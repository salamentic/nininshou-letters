After waiting for the snow to melt and watching the earth to crawl out, my inner serpent saw a whole lot of things. Surely, “to see” and “to know” are two things that are equally boundless—my whole body takes in the winds that sweep through the mountains, the sunlight shining through the blades of grass, and the cold of the river's waters; that way I begin to feel as if I know everything about spring. In the mornings, I glide over the bluebells that sprouted from the forest while sunbathing. As I imagine all this, somehow I feel that there is something more I need to feel. Perhaps because I remembered Yuan Zhen's poem. As I recited that poem, my own inner serpent felt if there were an open gap at the center of the spring clouds. It seems summer will end soon. Little by little, I began to hear the voices of the cicadas more clearly. Take care.

It is written as “evening cicadas”, but the "evening" is not necessary.
