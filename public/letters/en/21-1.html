September 16

What kind of person are you, Sensei? What did you do to end up in that place? Something really bad? Please tell me.

I committed theft—though officially, the charge was robbery causing bodily injury. I broke into my neighbor's house, shattered a window, and took some hidden cash. As I tried to leave, I ran into them. In my panic, I pushed past them and seriously injured them. A neighbor called the police, and I was arrested soon after. At the time, I had lost my job and was deeply addicted to alcohol. The reason I committed the theft was purely for the money.

September 22

Why did you need money, Sensei? Please tell me.

That was because I was weak. Even before my dependence on alcohol, I had been dependent on books. While I was working as a faculty member at a university, I turned to books to release the buildup of frustration I had whenever I got into conflict with others and to find an outlet for my emotions. I read books, absorbed the knowledge inside, and by storing all that up, I came to think that I was superior to others. To me, reading books wasn't so I could appreciate the beauty of literature and poetry. It was my own ugly pedantry that kept driving me to know more and more.
