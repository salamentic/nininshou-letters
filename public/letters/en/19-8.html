I see you looked up “Thoughts on Separation.” Thank you. Its official title is “Thoughts on Separation: Five Poems”; it consists of five poems, each with four lines of seven characters, and this is the fourth poem in the collection. It's also worth mentioning that this poem references Mencius.

  孟子曰、孔子登東山而小魯、登太山而小天下   故觀於海者難為水、游於聖人之門者難為言

  Mencius said:   When Confucius climbed Mount Dong, Lu seemed small.   When he climbed Mount Tai, the whole world seemed small.   Those who have seen the ocean will hardly be satisfied with water.   Those who have had the opportunity to be with a Sage will hardly be satisfied with a mere conversation."

A loose translation of the second half would be:   Those who have seen the ocean will believe that even a river is nothing more than a little water;   Those who have learned from a Sage will generally believe that a common opinion is of no importance.

Yuan Zhen recited a love poem for his deceased wife, using the same imagery as Confucius looking out over the world from a mountain. On the other hand, “The Clouds of Mount Wu” is a quote from “Rain Clouds over Mount Wu” in the Gao Tang Fu, which tells of a man and a woman making love in their dreams.
