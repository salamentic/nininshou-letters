My grandfather lives alone in a spacious house. I don't have a grandmother. A few years ago, she suffered a serious injury, which led to her spending the rest of her life in the hospital, and she passed away shortly after. Since then, my grandfather has always seemed lonely. The first time I sent you a letter, I included the poem “Turn Into a Cloud”. Sensei, you had said that it makes you imagine the countryside sea. That is the landscape I saw in the countryside where my grandfather lives. That came to mind when I was reading a book about clouds that was in my mother’s study room. When I leave my grandfather’s house, there are field trails. I used to walk there a lot with my grandmother. Beyond the field trails, clouds hang in the sky. They are smooth white summer clouds. I often used to tell my grandmother, “I want to turn into a cloud.” I think it's because they looked as if they were enjoying so much swimming in the blue sea. Even now, I still think that way a lot. I wrote that because I somehow remembered that moment. I left you a photo of the clouds. To tell you the truth, the last time I grabbed my mother’s camera and went for a walk, I was able to take a photo of some very beautiful clouds; my real intention was to send this to you. Everything else in this letter are just excuses. I plan to go to the library again this week. Please take care of yourself.

Sincerely,
