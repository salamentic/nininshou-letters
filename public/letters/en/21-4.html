In my opinion, the concepts of good and evil are simple to dichotomize. What is "evil" is the use of violence. Whether or not you do it with malice, you should never hurt someone. That's just something you should never do. If you do, you must never be forgiven. That's just reality. If you somehow think that's forgivable, the one committing the sin is you, plain and simple. I don't believe in the saying that everyone should one day be forgiven. If you were in the victim's position, you might not be able to believe such things, either. Was there not a discussion on good will in The Critique of Judgment? In another work, Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals, Kant says:      "Of all things that are considered 'good,'      the only thing that is unconditionally good      is a 'good will.'" According to Kant, there are several types of goods. Traits like disposition, courage, wisdom, or judgment are nothing but secondary goods. What makes me who I am are those exact wills.
