Earlier, you sent me the poem titled Shura. When I first read it, my first impression was “This is me”. It was as though someone was pointing a finger directly at my chest. I am often tormented by my sense of guilt. This is not a matter of being forgiven or atoning for sins. What I am afraid of is the possibility of no longer feeling this sense of guilt. What truly scares me is the fact that one day I might be forgiven for my crimes. That one day, thanks to the societal systems of penalty and imprisonment, my crimes may be forgiven. I did something that deserves no forgiveness. The sentences deemed out by the justice system are not an absolute standard. There’s no way that I should be forgiven just because I was taken out of society for some arbitrary period of time. I am not who you think I am. I am just another person who committed a crime. If I’m being honest, someone like me has no right to be exchanging letters with you like this. I have no right to be presumptiously lecturing you like this, playing pretend at being a teacher. I am sorry that I had to convey everything in such a deceptive manner. I truly am sorry.
