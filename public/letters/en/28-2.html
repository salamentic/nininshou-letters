Only after I began sending my poems to you did I see my own work objectively. Would it be embarrassing to show it to someone? Were my words used correctly? Were my techniques clumsy? Only when I became conscious of showing my work to another person did I begin to perceive my own writing. For the first time, I saw myself there. Sensei, the reason I was weak was because I kept avoiding showing my work to others. Because I had only been hiding the anger in the pit of my stomach. Because by putting myself down in a self-deprecating way, I was merely trying to seem special. Because I kept avoiding directly seeing that weakness. Yesterday, for the first time, I wrote a poem using only my own words. No theme, no quotation. Just an ordinary poem. Something almost embarrassing to show anyone, truly plain, like prose that anyone could write. Writing using only my own words is difficult. Even so, I feel that continuing to do this may be what I need right now. So, Mom... I'm okay now. You're still reading this letter, aren't you?
