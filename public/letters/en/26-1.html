Dear Sensei, I saw a patch of white-colored silver grass by the roadside. Even though we're halfway through autumn by now, the sun still shines so strongly and the piercing wind keeps on blowing, so I was looking back on the past summer for a bit. How have you been doing, Sensei? There have been some small changes in my life while writing this song. For the first time in a really long time, I used the train. Heading towards school. When I stepped out into the rotary in front of the station, I was hit by a strange feeling. Ah, well, not strange, but it's good to put it into words: my shoulders eased up. I hadn't been there in such a long time, yet not a single thing changed. How should I put this? In this town that I'd once thought was developing at such a fast pace — this single place had stayed exactly the same as it was back then. Then, the wind came blowing. My hair touched the side of my face and it felt a little itchy. That poem by Kenji Miyazawa came to mind. It's a piece I came to learn and love since I started regularly visiting the library. "The wind beckons outside." I couldn't hold it in anymore so I took out the memo pad I'd placed inside my bag. Of course, I wanted to write a poem.
