Dear Sensei, Since winter just began, the days have been gradually growing shorter. It's been quite some time since I last received a letter from you. How have you been, Sensei? Today, this is an important letter. I have the feeling that the letters I send to you may soon come to an end. Since then, I've gradually been going to the library less often. Perhaps it's because your words had lodged themselves somewhere deep in the back of my mind. The story of the sea of sand. Words are just tiny grains of sand, and their immeasurably vast gathering becomes context. The world is made of quotations, and yet quotations are only a part of the curtain. Technique is merely one tool. It must not become the goal itself. In the end, it is nothing more than ink at the tip of a pen. Now I think I understand, at least a little, why you have devoted yourself to simply receiving my poems. And also why, at the very beginning, you spoke about the soushou haiku tradition. Sensei, you are my mirror. I finally understand.
