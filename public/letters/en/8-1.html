Dear Sensei, Today it's just a letter, sorry, I haven't been able to write lately. That's why I've been going on a lot of walks, looking for something to write about. Now that I think of it, sensei, what is that mark stamped on the lower right corner of the letters you send me? That flower. I thought writing your name at the end was normal, but you can also put a stamp there instead, as if it were a signature, right? Actually, I guess I like that you stamp the bottom of the paper and send my letters and poems back to me. Yesterday, it seemed to me that my mother had a phone call with my grandfather. Once summer ends, my mother's schedule will lighten a bit and she has plans to go visit my grandfather at his house. It worries me that I have to go too. My grandfather's house is a bit out in the countryside. On the other hand, the place where I live is a small town. Oh, the address will be written on the envelope, so you'll know.To get to where my grandfather lives, I have to pass through several prefectures, so I can only see him a few times a year. For the past few years we can only visit him during New Year's, probably because my mother is pretty busy. I heard that my mother went to Tokyo and married my father. That's why I haven't met almost any of my relatives. There might not be anyone I remember. But I know my mother has a younger brother. The other day I heard that it was that younger brother who gave my mother the camera and the guitar.
