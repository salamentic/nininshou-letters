之

不知周之夢為胡蝶与、胡蝶夢為周与 “I do not know whether it was Zhou who dreamed he was a butterfly, or a butterfly dreaming that it was Zhou.” Zhou, of course, being Zhuang Zhou. That was his real name. (The character zi is a term of respect, like 'Master,' so Zhuangzi can also be called Master Zhuang.) Anyways, one day, Zhou dreamed that he had become a butterfly. In the body of a butterfly, he could enjoy himself as he pleased, feeling utterly comfortable. Being a butterfly seemed entirely natural, so he forgot that he was human. Then, when he awoke, he was Zhou again — a human once more. Am I actually dreaming that I have become a butterfly, or am I a butterfly dreaming about the life of a human? That's what that is about. If we look at it like this, we can see where Sakutaro got his influences from. In more contemporary language, he was paying “homage” to Zhou.
