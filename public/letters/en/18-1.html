Dear Sensei, It continues to be really hot here. While I was at the library this Wednesday, I happened to read a book about hot air balloons. Do you know how hot air balloons work, Sensei? When air heats up, it becomes lighter than cold air. You told me about this before, didn't you? As a result, the fabric of the hot air balloon catches the rising hot air, carrying both, the fabric as well as the attached cargo upwards. To be honest, before I looked into all of this, all of it felt a bit like magic to me. Realizing how simple the mechanics really were, I felt a little sad. Even though this was something I could have figured out had I given it some thought. I think that the hot air balloon is very similar to my desire for knowledge. Just like how a hot air balloon's only goal is to endlessly rise upwards, my desire for knowledge is the same in that it's endless. The fact that there's a limit to how much I can learn in my lifetime is also quite similar to a hot air balloon. After all, a hot air balloon can never make it to space. Maybe I want to make it to space. You told me this in one of your replies in the past right Sensei? That human beings are shaped by their experiences. Because humans are able to know, because we have daily experiences, the mind accumulates the world through the five senses. If that's the case, not wanting to know more is the same as being dead isn't it? Not wanting to learn more about something new is the same as stagnation. A stagnant river is no longer a river. It has turned itself into ordinary water. With no destination in sight, having come to a complete standstill, it is nothing but stagnating water.
