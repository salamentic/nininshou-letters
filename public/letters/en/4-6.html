You were probably just looking for a theme; in that sense, not having one makes things simpler. There’s something good in cases like these, too. I thought those two crossed-out lines were desirable inflections. Of course, my opinion might not be the best fit for the image of your work. As long as you just listen to what I’m saying, I think those two lines are what’s important to you, whether you cross it out or leave it in. P.S.: If looking for a theme stresses you out, try writing something using the word “sun” next time. If you put on a pair of shoes you don’t usually wear and go outside, you’ll have a different feeling than you always do, don’t you think? Writing about a theme someone else gives you is a good thing as well. Poetry is like a journey.
