I have always believed that I must not be forgiven. To be honest, that feeling has not changed even now. Even if you, your grandfather, your grandmother, and your mother were to say such things, I cannot forgive myself. When your mother first brought me the idea of exchanging letters with you, I hesitated. I said that someday you would inevitably learn who I was, and that when that time came, no one could know how deeply it might wound you, and that I would not be able to bear that responsibility. But as we continued meeting again and again, something in me began to waver. If you were to ask whether there was, somewhere inside me, a wish to lighten the burden of my guilt, I could not easily answer. It is true that our exchange began as a kind of atonement. But this I can say from the bottom of my heart: I truly love your poetry. All this time, I have seen a light in your poems. Within my life... Within the days I spent trembling under the weight of my guilt, your poems shone like a gentle light breaking through. A flawless light that shines fiercely in the darkness of night.
