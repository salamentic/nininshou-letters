Dear Sensei I'm sorry for sending two letters in a row. I finished reading Zhuangzi. Along with it, I also read Confucius. I borrowed several books by Shimazaki Touson. I borrowed other poetry collections by Sakutaro too. Since I became interested in waka from the Heian period, I borrowed the Kokin Wakashu and the Shika Wakashu as well. Libraries are amazing. Until you gave me a reason, Sensei, I had never gone to a library outside of school before, so this feels very new to me. My mother’s library card was lying in the living room, so I borrowed it, and lately I’ve practically been living at the library. Other people's words, how wonderful they are. My own words have never stirred my heart, but simply because someone else wrote them, they can feel like something entirely different. With this much to read, I feel like something new might come out of it again. And when you gave me the theme “The Sun” the other day, it really helped me. Somehow it made me happy. If you don’t mind, could you give me another theme sometime? Also, if there are other books I should read, I would be very happy if you could tell me about them. I think I'll be spending a lot of time shut away in the library from now on.
