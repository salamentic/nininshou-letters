Dear Sensei Sensei, do you smoke? As I wrote before, my mother is a smoker. The other day, I saw my mother walking down the street at night. She was on her way home from work. visible from my room. She walked past our home, and took a cigarette out of her purse. Then, she began walking under the moon, walking further and further away from home. With her silhouette growing smaller, I could see a small red glow of the flame in her right hand. The street light above her head was out, and in the dim darkness, a floating ball of fire glowed. I found myself thinking that perhaps this is how will-o'-the-wisps are born. My mother always takes a detour before coming home. Yesterday, I took my mother's cigarettes and matches and then went outside my home. When I tore open the pack, I smelled a scent of spice. With my right thumb, I open the lid of the pack. The top of the lid flipped back. Putting aside the thin paper inside, I saw the cylindrical cigarettes, aligned neatly in a row. Like a honeycomb. The base contained a spongy substance, and the tip was stuffed with a fine-crinkled brown grass.
