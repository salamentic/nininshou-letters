April 14 Hello.

Ah, that's right, it's not about just “playing”, but about “playing a role” isn't it. That’s a little embarrassing.   The parts I've crossed out are parts that make me anxious. In my imagination, this poem has been about myself, going out on weekdays while in good health. While kids my age are learning all sorts of things, I’m wandering around the city with hardly anyone else around. Sensei, how did things go for you back when you were in school? Enjoying an afternoon out on a sick day, it feels like getting away with something. Yet, at the same time, getting that strange feeling of being left behind. Did you ever feel that way? I feel that way quite often these days. It could even be interpreted as a universal feeling, maybe it’s just feeling intoxicated by the differences in daily lives.   Put that way, if it were just a poem about going out for a walk while pretending to be sick, I feel like the lines I crossed out are unnecessary. My intention with the part “We were in love with” was to refer to the times when I was living a normal life. It’s about what it means to be normal. I still don’t know exactly what I’m in love with, but maybe it’s closer to the expression “longing for something”. But I’m still not sure about what I should do with that part.
