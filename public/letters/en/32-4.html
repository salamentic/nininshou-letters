Truthfully, it doesn’t really matter. What you were thinking, what you wanted to do to me, what the appropriate punishment for the crime you’ve committed, none of it. What is in my hand is the result, the number of these letters, their weight. There’s probably nothing else that determines the value of the time I’ve spent. I’m not sure how to put it. It’s just simply the result. The weight of this envelope in front of my eyes, the poems I’ve written for the past 9 months, without you Sensei, this will never come to being. So, either to forgive or not to forgive, that doesn’t matter. This alone is what remains. So Sensei, thank you. The one who forgives Sensei is not the society, nor a person, but this bundle of letters. I think I’ll let go of all our correspondence letters now. I might gather them into an envelope, label them clearly with dates and numbers, and leave them
