P.S.:  Regarding the handwriting form, there’s a certain way to use Genkouyoushi. The basic rule is one character per square, but in your case, I imagine you ignore the squares so you don’t run out of paper so quickly. I can totally relate, when I was a kid, I used to manage my allowance the same way when I was running low on money. I’ll also write my letters trying to use as little paper as possible. On the other hand, since your letters begin with “Dear Sensei” and end with “Sincerely,” adding a seasonal greeting at the beginning would make your writing more refined. However, making things more formal might make you feel more awkward. You can write the manner you like to, I’ll be impatiently waiting to receive another letter from you.
