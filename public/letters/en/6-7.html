Similarly related to "To Dream of a Butterfly" is the Heian Tanka poet Ooe no Masafusa's most recognizable work:   Having dwelled on the flowers for a hundred years, this world has indeed existed as a fleeting dream.

I have lived, dwelling on the flowers for a long time, but this world was, in fact, a butterfly's dream. That’s what the poem means. On the other hand, we also have Shimazaki Touson. This is really where the "Dream of the Butterfly" originated.   It is truly delightful to call this life a journey, even as a butterfly dreaming of being human. Lots of people have thrown in their two cents on this topic, so this is only a fraction of what's out there. Finally, I want to share that Shimazaki Touson also has his own "Sun" poem. I remember this especially well—and this may not be entirely accurate, but: Anyone can become the sun. What should be important to us is not to chase after the sun that we can see with our own two eyes, but to hoist the sun within ourselves. Please send me more of your poems.

Shimozaki Touson, "The Words of the Sun" I have waited over thirty years. I will probably live the rest of my days out like this, waiting for the sun to rise. But anyone can become the sun. It is imperative for us not to chase after the sun we can see with our own two eyes, but to hoist the sun high within ourselves.
