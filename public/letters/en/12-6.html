I read your poem. It’s a wonderful poem, isn’t it? The words “forget me please” are conventionally used to convey sorrow, but for some reason they convey a feeling of “looking forward” in your writing. I am sure you never meant for this to be a sad song, right? I quite like Hakushu Kitahara too. I took a look at the picture you sent as well. The title of this book is not “Paulownia of Flowers”, it’s actually read as “Flowers of Paulownia”. The Paulownia is a tree that bears beautiful purple flowers, a lot like the Wisteria. People in the Taisho period were a bit different from us in that when it came to horizontal writing, they chose to write from right to left. Thus the actual title is "Flowers of Paulownia". It’s confusing isn’t it? Do you know the reason why Hakushu wrote this poem? Back when he was a kid, he happened to contract Typhoid. His wet nurse contracted the same from him and ended up passing away as a result. The loquat tree is often known as “The substitute tree”. If we take this into account, you probably see what Hakushu was truly trying to say in writing this poem. The poem takes on a completely whole new meaning.
