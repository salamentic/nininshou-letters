Dear Sensei, There is something I must apologize for. Yesterday, I woke up unusually early and checked the envelope that had arrived in the mailbox. And the envelope from you yesterday seemed different from the usual ones. The address was definitely mine, but something about it was different. First of all, the envelope was vertical. It was the common brown envelope you see everywhere. The ones I usually receive are white, horizontal Western-style envelopes. When I turned it over, I saw a kind of stamp pressed along the seal. It was that same flower mark that is always at the edge of your letters. And for some reason, yesterday's envelope had your return address written on it. You never write your own address on the envelope, do you? As long as my address, the destination, is written, the letter will arrive, so I assumed you simply omitted it and didn't think much about it… To tell the truth, this is the second time I've seen an envelope like this. The last time was when you sent that message that said, “You're reading this now, aren't you?” I found that one early in the morning in the mailbox too. That brown envelope looked very much like this one. Just like now, it arrived on Saturday. A day earlier than usual.
