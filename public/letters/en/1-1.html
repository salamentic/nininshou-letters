Dear Sir/Madam. I saw your flyer. If it’s okay with you, would you be able to look over my work for me? I write poems. When the start of each month rolls around, I buy a 50-page stack of Genkouyoushi at my local stationary store. It’s so I can put my writings in them. I have Genkouyoushi papers that I’ve written on scattered about all over in my room. A lot of them, at that. At the end of each week, my mother tells me to clean them up, so I put them away into a box, which has become a habit of sorts. On the back of your leaflet, it said to write something about myself along with my works, so I did so, but I can only really write about this much. I don’t attend school. That’s why I spend my free time absorbed in words. And around when the sunset’s colour starts to appear outside the window, I can hear the sound of the school bell, chiming across the city with the voices of everybody happily returning home. Somehow, I end up kind of feeling as though I have nothing at all.
