September 22

Dear Sensei, I'm still trying to decide how I should react to this. They often say to separate the art from the artist. But I wonder where we end and where the work begins. Art museums are essentially exhibiting the private diaries of artists, aren't they? Even our letters—the moment it meets a third party's eyes—might become something that is subjected to aesthetic judgment and required to serve the public interest, don't you think? I can't draw the line between fiction and reality. I wonder what it is I'm trying to say. I guess I want to try and separate your words from your crimes. But maybe I'm only free to say that because I'm not a victim of your crimes myself. But I get that your personality has nothing to do with the words that you write for me and the things that you teach me. The other day, I read Immanuel Kant's “Critique of Judgement.” In it, I took the idea to be that a person's character should be set aside, and that something ought to be evaluated solely based on the observer's aesthetic judgment. Or maybe that's just how I want to see it…… I'll send more of my poems. And if it's okay with you, please give me a reply. As if nothing ever happened.
