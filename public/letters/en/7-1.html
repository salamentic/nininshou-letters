Dear Sensei, It drizzled a little today. How have you been doing Sensei? I often take inspiration from my surroundings when it comes to the themes I choose. When I say environment, I mean things like the scenery, sounds, things I experience, information that enters my field of view. Essentially, stuff that surrounds me. The day before yesterday, the TV in the living room was kept turned on, showing the news. My mother turns the TV on to watch the news but she always forgets to turn it back off before leaving for work. While eating breakfast alone, I listen to it absentmindedly. As I sit there, I am transported to the skies of a faraway land. I heard the news mention the word bomb. And so I imagine. On one side of a massive wall, pillars of smoke rise from the prayer-like flames of war. On the other side, the usual hustle-bustle of an ordinary civilization carries on. As I listen to the TV, I turn into a bird, gliding above, watching it all unfold. The newspaper, TV, radio, people’s voices - together, they shape my vision. Then, my mind wanders to the concept of good and evil. I know that it isn’t a simple dichotomy, but I do end up imagining how I’d judge which side is evil. This kind of thing is impossible to understand without putting yourself in the shoes of the victim or the perpetrator, isn’t it
