Dear Sensei, Spring is about to end. Sensei, how have you been holding up? The other day, I suddenly woke up in the middle of the night. I couldn't bring myself back to sleep, so I went outside. The national highway at night was entirely stranded, and just the signal lights were left glowing. It was so quiet, as if the city had died. So I screamed. Just once—from the bottom of my chest—really loudly. And when I looked to the side, I saw my reflection in the glass of a pitch-black storefront. My body was bent forward in a strange way. I was somewhat afraid of that image, so I came home and fell asleep. The next morning, I noticed a cigarette on top of the newspaper in the living room. It was the brand my mother always smokes. Seeing that, I suddenly had this overwhelming urge to grab my pen. I came back to my room and wrote a poem on a sheet of Genkouyoshi. That's what this is. The "Post" in the title is the kind of post in, say, "post-modern." Spring is about to end, so you could say it's post-spring right now. It's that empty, transitional season before summer comes. The world moves at unthinkable speeds and I can hear the sound of society and the environment ripening along with the seasons.
