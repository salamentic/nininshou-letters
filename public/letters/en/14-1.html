Dear Sensei,  I found a Crepe Myrtle bud by the side of the road. It seems summer is getting closer, isn't it? Sensei, what do you think of when you hear the word “Martian”? In my case, humans come to mind. They aren’t octopus-like creatures like in H. G. Wells’ “The War of the Worlds.”  I wrote about myself in today’s poem. It’s about my inferiority complex. I wasn't always like this. If I had to put it simply, I used to be quite arrogant as a child, though easy-to-hurt as a result. Yet despite that, I immersed myself in a feeling of omnipotence as if I could do anything or be anywhere. Before I knew it, I ended up like this.  Mars is a symbol of longing. In this poem, that would be Sakutaro. And more broadly, it’s everything. All the people besides me who can write wonderful words. People like you, Sensei, those who can draw on knowledge without preparation, those who "possess" it. If that is Mars, then the opposite is Earth, this ordinary ground. I, too, once thought of myself as a Martian. I really imagined as if I were somewhere special, like Mars, and possessed something special.
