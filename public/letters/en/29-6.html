August 9 (1)

Ahhh, that does seem to be the case, doesn’t it? It seems like your mother is reading these letters, isn’t she? Probably without telling you either. Further, it seems like my letters are being taken out as well. That explains why it felt like there were parts of my replies that you never ended up reading. I did not want to believe it to be true, but no matter how many times I wrote the exact same thing, I never received a reply for anything related to the crime I committed. I finally understand now. When you do end up learning about all of this, I am sure you will feel like you have been tricked. I am sorry. But, I leave the responsibility of judgement to your mother. As you will eventually come to realize, I am a person who committed the crime that I committed, so perhaps it is in in your best interest to be kept in the dark about this after all. That said, just in case this does end up reaching you, I will write this once again. Your poems are wonderful. I really want to listen to them along with the actual melody. To be honest, being pessimistic is extremely easy to do. It’s not an easy thing to stop. You might not be able to stop even if you gave it your whole life trying. After all, it lets you get satisfaction out of being sadistic towards yourself, with almost a drug-like intensity.
