Dear Sensei, After sending you the poem about the hot air balloon the other day, I've been thinking about it for quite some time. It's about the desire for knowledge. I wrote in that letter that “to know” is like the ascent of a hot air balloon. I thought that if that's the case, then that “desire to know things” that overflows from within me must be “to think,” doesn't it? That is the opposite of “to know.” “To know” is the result of an action, while “to want to know” is what comes from the heart. If I were to investigate this line of thought deeply, perhaps I will understand my true nature. That's the reason I plan to read various poems from around the world, not just in my native language, so that I may broaden my horizons. Last week I managed to read poetry from overseas. What I read last week is a collection of poems gathered from the Tang Dynasty. In doing so, I found “Thoughts on Separation” by Yuan Zhen in that collection, which you had taught me in a previous letter. 曾经沧海难为水 除却巫山不是云
