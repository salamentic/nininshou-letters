Misreading of “Flowers of Paulownia”?

Dear Sensei, The loquats in my neighborhood have become ready. How have you been doing Sensei?

ripe

I found the loquats on Tuesday while on my way home from the library. It’s a fresh orange colored, round fruit. I couldn’t get close to them since they were across a hedge, but those orange spots hiding among the green leaves instantly caught my eye. At that exact moment, I happened to be reading Hakushu Kitahara’s ”Paulownia of Flowers”, and as if it were fated, my eyes fell upon the word loquat on the page that I was reading. It was a Tanka that went like this: “On the loquat tree, a golden fruit shined, Startled, I jumped backwards.” “To jump backwards”, seems to mean to tumble backwards and fall on your back. I don’t think I’ve ever jumped and fallen over in that fashion. That said, in that moment where I saw the words loquat on the corner of the page, right after having seen the loquat fruit in real life, I remember feeling this sense of joy. It felt as though in that moment, the world, the letters and the palm of my hand had all become loosely connected. To put it simply, it felt like fate.
