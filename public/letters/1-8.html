From those origins, it became a color called “cotton-white.” Taking all that into account, the lyric "Even the moon that ought to shine is hidden, and only your white sleeve glows," made me imagine the moon concealed in the darkness of night where a white sleeve floats visibly in the sky. If we think about it this way, perhaps throughout the whole poem the word “you” is actually indicating the color white itself. About “Turn Into a Cloud” I could see a picture of clouds, as if suspended above the summer field trails. And at the same time, the countryside sea came to my mind for some reason. I wonder why this is. It’s probably because I strongly associate field trails with rural seasides. Perhaps my hometown left a big impression on me.
